NEET

  • NEET is abbreviated for Natinal Eligibolity cum Examination Test(Undergraduate) also called NEET(UG), is entrance examination needed to clear for anyone intrested in persuing a bachelore level education in field of mesicine.
  • The examination is conducted by NTA(National Testing Agency).
  • NEET in 2021 will be held with not be held with any reduced syllabus.But there has bbeen slight delay in examination due to postpnement of CBSE examination.
  • There is possibility that NEET might be conducted twice a year. But nbo any official conformation has been yet made.
  • Through NEET examination 82,950 MBBS(Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery), 26,949 BDS(Bachelor of Dental Surgery), 52,720 AYUSH,525 BVSSc(Bachelor of Veterinary Science ) and A.H(Animal Health) seats including 18889 AIIMS(All India Institutes of Medical Sciences) and 249 JIPMER(Jawaharlal Institute of Postgraduate Medical Education & Research) seats will be granted.
    Fun fact: Actually the abbreviation of MBBS has been derived from Latin where MBBS stands for Medicinae Baccalaureus Baccalaureus Chirurigiae
  • NEET also conducts entrance examination for post graduate medical cources like M.S and M.D
  • NEET is a single stage examination held ones a year offline with pen and paper.
  • NEET is conducted for three hours

Eligibility criteria for NEET

Age:

Candidates should be of minimum 17 years at the time of examinationor by December 31 2021.
There is no upper age limit. NOTE :Any students who are above 25 years please make double conformation from the officials.

Educational qualification:

Candidates must have to pass Physics,Chemistry,Biology/Biotechnology and English

Candidates must have passed class 12 or equivalent like Alevels or so from a recognized university.

Candidates must also secure atleast 50 percentile in NEET examination to take admission in medical campus even in pay basis.
